Interruptions, 1968. By Vera Molnar

Molnar was one of the first artists to experiment with using computers to create drawings.

To create this piece, she gained access to an IBM computer at a university in Paris in 1968. Using a basic program of her own creation, she blindly punched data into a card and printed the results onto a plotter, resulting in this abstract piece--which was abhored by the artistic community at the time.
